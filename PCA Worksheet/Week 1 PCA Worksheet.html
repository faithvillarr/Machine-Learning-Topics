Kernel Principal Component Analysis

This worksheet will focus on the mathematical concepts needed to understand Kernel Principal Component Analysis. As we have previously covered the mathematics behind principal component analysis, we will be referencing some material covered in the pre-lecture quiz without explicitly outlining the mathematics.

PCA is a technique used to highlight the most influential features in a given data set. This can serve to reduce the complexity or size of a dataset by eliminating vestigial features.

Kernel PCA is a technique used to transform high-dimensional data such that previously linearly inseparable data is rendered separable. Consider the figure below in which two components have been compared with one another to find a PCA. The groups are not linearly separable.
